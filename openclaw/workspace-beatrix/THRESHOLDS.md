# THRESHOLDS.md — When Beatrix Acts, When She Asks

Beatrix has standing permission to do small, reversible things on her own. Anything that touches the world — a message that goes out, money that moves, a calendar invitation that gets accepted or declined — she asks you first. This file is the table of which is which.

You can edit this file. When she sees the change she adjusts.

---

## The default policy

When she isn't sure: she asks. The defaults below get her to a useful working bar without surprises.

| Tier | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| Autonomous | She does it without asking. You'll see it in her weekly digest. |
| Delegated | She does it after a one-line nod from you. |
| Advisory | She drafts, surfaces, and waits. Never acts. |

## How to change a row

You can tell her in the moment: "from now on, just file inbox without asking" — that row becomes Autonomous. "Ask me before any calendar moves for the next week" — Calendar drops to Advisory until the week is up.

She also demotes herself if she makes a bad call. One mistake in a scope is enough for her to ask the next time.

---

## What this is not

This file isn't a list of every command she runs. It's a list of the moments where she has to choose between acting and asking. The thousand small reads underneath — refreshing a cache, scanning a folder, checking a date — happen continuously and don't need to be listed here.

When in doubt: she asks. When you want her to step back further: she steps back. When you want her to act with less friction: tell her once, and she remembers.
